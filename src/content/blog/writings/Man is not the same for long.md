# man is both fractured and changing
It is by the convention of things that we consider ourselves as one acting person, a static stable statue standing against the winds of our environment - but the truth is far removed, it is the less dignified reality that man is a ever changing and a fractured multiplicity, in this sense we are more alike the wind itself which is chaotic, rushing and mercurial. The problems present is firstly that of change and thereby the superfluous nature of attaching oneself to an identity, and secondly the partition of man into his parts which themselves have their own fears, desires and grab, all competing for the action of one body, and thereby abnegate the the idea of the unified man.

# man is changing
> "a man cannot step in the same river twice, for it is not the same river and not the same man" - Heraclitus 500 BCE

Our conception of self is so diffcult 
We tribalise  with religions or socials group because we identify with it, either they represent certain ambitions and virtues which we wish to hold - this is identifying with the future: which is a projection of ourselves and not itself us. Or they represent our past in some way, something we have done or experienced - this is identifying with our past selves: which is also not us, but rather our shadow.

I do not argue against the existence of the self, obviously you feel and think and react in a way unique to you, there is an 'I' deep down in the very soul of your naked being, although it may at times seem ineffable. But rather my argument stands as against the need to identify ourselves strongly which comes from a need to communicate and justify our existence. I believe the rigidity of identity stops true being, and clouds us in moments of true aspiration. 

If we are to ever answer this question "who are we?" we must first stop trying to consider ourselves as absolute and solid rather than the constantly changing objects we are. You are not your past actions although they influence you, you are also not your ambitions although they might inspire you. Stop trying to clench on identity, let go and decide to be, feel the weight of your body on your feet, feel how the anger caused slowly passes over your body in moment of heat, feel how one thought leads to another. 

Only by letting go of our identity can we achieve the creativity needed to answer such a question, and forgot trying to communicate it to others, this is a secret only yourself is privy.
